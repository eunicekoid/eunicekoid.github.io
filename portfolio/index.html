Forecasting Forest Coverage Impacts on Air Quality and Covid-19 Transmission

Preliminary evidence in the United States (as of mid 2020) suggested that environmental factors such as forest density and air quality may have an impact on Covid-19 transmission and deaths. Using open source data, I employ machine learning techniques to predict Covid-19 cases and deaths in India from forest coverage and air quality data. The model's prediction suggests that higher populated and polluted states will likely have higher cases and deaths from Covid-19.

Predicting Crime Rates in The Hague Using Machine Learning

I use spatial analysis, regression, and clustering algorithms to predict crime rates based on key features such as unemployment rate, disposable income, number of business and educational establishments, and number of private cars per address.

Modeling Preferential Flow through Unsaturated Soil using the 1-D Dual Porosity Approach

I created a model that expands upon the Richards Equation for the movement of water through unsaturated soils to include preferential flow.

Understanding Sedimentation Trajectories with Agent-based Modeling of Sudan's Gezira Irrigation Scheme

I developed an agent-based model incorporating landscape-driven effects, hydrological processes, and sediment deposition to simulate environmental conditions and maintenance strategies over the last century. From the model's results, I draw conclusions on the principle cause of sedimentation in the Gezira canals.
